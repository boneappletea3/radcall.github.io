What Is an Ultrasound-Guided Breast Biopsy?

What Is an Ultrasound-Guided Breast Biopsy?

An ultrasound-guided breast biopsy is a minimally invasive procedure that uses ultrasound imaging (sound waves) to guide a needle to a specific area in your breast to remove small tissue samples for testing.[1][2] This procedure is performed when a suspicious finding is visible on ultrasound imaging.

Ultrasound-guided biopsy is the preferred method for evaluating masses (lumps) that can be seen on ultrasound because it allows your doctor to watch the needle in real-time as it enters the breast tissue.[3][4]

Why Ultrasound Guidance?

Ultrasound guidance offers several advantages:[3][4]

• Real-time visualization: Your doctor can see the needle and the target area continuously during the procedure

• No radiation exposure: Unlike mammography, ultrasound uses sound waves, not X-rays

• Comfortable positioning: You typically lie on your back or side, rather than having your breast compressed

• Accessibility: Can reach areas that may be difficult to access with other methods

• Shorter procedure time: Usually takes 15-30 minutes

What Happens During the Procedure?

Before the biopsy:[1][3]

• You will lie on your back or side on an examination table

• The radiologist will use ultrasound to locate the area of concern

• The skin over the biopsy site will be cleaned with an antiseptic solution

• A local anesthetic (numbing medicine) will be injected into the area—you may feel a brief sting or pinch

During the biopsy:[1][2][3]

• Once the area is numb, a small nick (about 3-5 mm) may be made in the skin

• The doctor will insert a core biopsy needle (typically 14-gauge) into your breast while watching on the ultrasound screen

• You may feel pressure but should not feel sharp pain

• Multiple tissue samples (usually 3-6 cores) will be taken to ensure adequate sampling[2][3]

• A small titanium marker clip will be placed at the biopsy site to mark the location for future reference[2]

• The doctor will use ultrasound to confirm the marker is in the correct position

After sample collection:[1]

• Pressure will be applied to the biopsy site to stop any bleeding

• The area will be covered with a sterile bandage

• No stitches are typically needed

• You will usually have a mammogram right after the procedure to document the location of the marker clip for future reference[5]

• The entire procedure usually takes 15-30 minutes

Immediately after

• You can usually go home right away and resume most normal activities within 24 hours[3]

• Avoid strenuous activity or heavy lifting for 24-48 hours

• Keep the biopsy site clean and dry for 24 hours

Common side effects:[3][6]

• Bruising and tenderness are normal and may last several days to a week

• Mild swelling at the biopsy site

• Minor bleeding or spotting on your bandage

Pain management

• Over-the-counter pain relievers (such as acetaminophen) are usually sufficient

• Apply ice packs (wrapped in a towel) for 15-20 minutes at a time to reduce swelling

• Wear a supportive bra for comfort

When to Call Your Doctor

Contact your doctor if you experience:[3]

• Fever (temperature above 100.4°F or 38°C)

• Increasing pain that is not relieved by over-the-counter medication

• Excessive bleeding that soaks through your bandage

• Signs of infection: increasing redness, warmth, or pus at the biopsy site

• A large, firm lump at the biopsy site that continues to grow

When Will I Get My Results?

Biopsy results are typically available within 3-7 days.[1] Your doctor will contact you to discuss the results and next steps. If the results show cancer, you will be referred to a breast cancer specialist. If the results are benign (not cancer), your doctor will discuss the appropriate follow-up plan.[3]

Important Information About Medications

Blood thinners:[1]

Recent studies show that most patients can safely continue their blood-thinning medications during breast biopsy, as clinically significant bleeding complications are very rare (less than 0.5%).[1] However, you should:

• Tell your doctor about ALL medications you take, including aspirin, ibuprofen, and supplements

• Follow your doctor's specific instructions about which medications to continue or stop

• Never stop taking prescribed blood thinners without talking to your doctor first

How Accurate Is Ultrasound-Guided Biopsy?

Ultrasound-guided core needle biopsy is highly accurate, with success rates exceeding 99% when performed by experienced radiologists.[2] The procedure has been shown to be as accurate as surgical biopsy while being less invasive, less expensive, and causing minimal scarring.[3][4]

Questions to Ask Before Your Procedure

Remember

• Ultrasound-guided breast biopsy is a safe, well-established procedure with very low complication rates

• Most patients experience only minor bruising and tenderness

• The procedure provides accurate diagnosis while avoiding the need for surgery in most cases

• Your healthcare team is available to answer any questions or concerns you may have
