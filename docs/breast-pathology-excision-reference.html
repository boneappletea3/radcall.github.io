Overview

Quick, concordance-first management guidance after core needle biopsy.

v1.0
Workflow: Establish radiology–pathology concordance for every biopsy. Any discordant result warrants repeat sampling or excision [1]. If concordant, use the tables and lesion-specific criteria below to decide between surgical excision, vacuum-assisted excision (VAE), or imaging surveillance.
Radiology–pathology concordance Upgrade risk Selective excision MDT review

Excision recommended

Higher-risk lesions and discordant cases where upgrade risk or uncertainty is meaningful.

More aggressive
Pathology Upgrade rate Recommendation Notes Refs
Atypical ductal hyperplasia (ADH) 15–30% Excision Highest upgrade rate of common high-risk lesions [1][3][4]
Non-classic LCIS (pleomorphic / florid) Variable Excision with negative margins Limited outcomes data [4][5]
Papilloma with atypia ~11% Excision Includes ADH/ALH/LCIS within papilloma [5][6]
Fibroepithelial lesion (FEL) 25–35% to phyllodes Excision FA vs phyllodes cannot be reliably distinguished on CNB [8]
Any discordant biopsy result Variable Excision or repeat biopsy Pathology does not explain imaging finding [4]

Observation may be appropriate

Concordant cases with low upgrade risk; multidisciplinary review recommended.

Selective
Pathology Upgrade rate Criteria for observation Notes Refs
Atypical lobular hyperplasia (ALH) ~2% if concordant Concordant, adequately sampled Often managed with imaging surveillance [1][4]
Classic LCIS Low if concordant Concordant, not extensive, not associated with calcifications Enhanced screening [1][4]
Flat epithelial atypia (FEA) 1–7% (pure FEA) Small lesion removed at biopsy; no concurrent ADH; concordant; no genetic mutation; no prior breast cancer Observation only if strict criteria met [3][13][14][15]
Papilloma without atypia 1–4% Concordant; ≤1 cm; >50% removed; asymptomatic; age <60; no strong family history Selective observation [16][17][18][24]
Radial scar (without atypia) ~1–9% Concordant; adequately sampled; no associated atypia VAE may be alternative to open excision [9][19][21][22]
Reminder: “Observation” assumes concordance and adequate sampling. If discordant, escalate management [1].

Lesion-specific guidance

Accordion format for rapid scanning; each citation links to the bibliography.

Clinical nuance
ADH Default excision; selective observation in carefully chosen patients
Often excise
  • Standard: Surgical excision recommended [4]
  • Select observation may be considered if: limited ADH; near-complete calcification removal; concordant; no suspicious MRI enhancement [3][5]
  • After diagnosis: Enhanced screening per NCCN [1]
Lobular neoplasia (ALH / classic LCIS) Observation acceptable when concordant and not extensive; non-classic LCIS excise
Risk-based
  • ALH: Observation acceptable if concordant and adequately sampled [4]
  • Classic LCIS: Observation acceptable if concordant and not extensive [4]
  • Non-classic LCIS: Excision with negative margins recommended [1]
FEA Pure FEA has low upgrade; observation possible with strict criteria
Often observe
  • Pure FEA upgrade rate: depends on cohort; generally low [13][14]
  • Observation may be considered if: pure FEA, concordant, adequately sampled, lesion removed at biopsy, no major risk factors [3][15]
Intraductal papilloma With atypia excise; without atypia risk-stratify
Selective
  • With atypia: Excision recommended [6]
  • Without atypia — consider excision if: discordant, symptomatic, large, older age, incomplete removal [16]
  • Without atypia — observation acceptable if: concordant, small, largely removed, asymptomatic [17][18]
Radial scar / complex sclerosing lesion With atypia excise; without atypia consider observation if well sampled
Consider VAE
  • With atypia: Excision recommended [19]
  • Without atypia: Upgrade rate generally low when concordant and well-sampled [21][22]
  • VAE: May be alternative to open surgery in selected cases [9]

Key principles

What to emphasize in daily practice.

Essentials
  1. Concordance is critical: Radiology–pathology correlation for every biopsy [1]
  2. MDT review helps: High-risk lesion management benefits from conference review [5][23]
  3. Individualize decisions: Age, symptoms, family history, genetics, and prior breast cancer matter [3]
