Overview

Quick, concordance-first management guidance after core needle biopsy.

v1.0
How to use this: Start with concordance. If discordant, escalate (repeat biopsy or excision). If concordant, use the tables below and the lesion-specific criteria to decide between surgical excision, vacuum-assisted excision (VAE), or imaging surveillance [1].
Radiology–pathology concordance Upgrade rates Selective excision Multidisciplinary review

Excision recommended

High-risk lesions and discordant cases where upgrade risk or uncertainty is meaningful.

More aggressive
Pathology Upgrade rate Recommendation Notes Refs
Atypical ductal hyperplasia (ADH) 15–30% Excision Highest upgrade rate of common high-risk lesions [1][2][3]
Non-classic LCIS (pleomorphic / florid) Variable Excision with negative margins Limited outcomes data [4][5]
Papilloma with atypia ~11% Excision Includes ADH, ALH, or LCIS within papilloma [5][6]
Fibroepithelial lesion (FEL) 25–35% to phyllodes Excision FA vs phyllodes cannot be reliably distinguished on CNB [7]
FEL “suspicious for phyllodes” >75% (expert opinion) Excision Higher suspicion warrants excision [7]
Benign phyllodes tumor Continued growth risk Excision Potential for progression to malignant [8][9]
Any discordant biopsy result Variable Excision or repeat biopsy Pathology does not explain imaging finding [4]

Observation may be appropriate

Concordant cases with low upgrade risk; multidisciplinary review recommended.

Selective
Pathology Upgrade rate Criteria for observation Notes Refs
Classic LCIS Low if concordant Concordant, not associated with calcifications, not extensive Surveillance with enhanced screening [1][2]
Atypical lobular hyperplasia (ALH) ~2% if concordant Concordant, adequately sampled Often managed with imaging surveillance [1][3]
Flat epithelial atypia (FEA) 1–7% (pure FEA) Small lesion, completely removed at biopsy, no concurrent ADH, no genetic mutation, no prior breast cancer Consider observation only if all criteria met [4][5][6]
Papilloma without atypia 1–4% Concordant, ≤1 cm, >50% removed at biopsy, no symptoms, age <60, no family history Low-risk criteria allow observation [3][7][8]
Radial scar (without atypia) 1–9% Concordant, adequately sampled (>50%), no associated atypia VAE may be an alternative to open excision [9][10][11]
Fibroadenoma (concordant) Very low Concordant, no atypia, stable No routine excision needed [12]
Reminder: “Observation” here assumes radiology–pathology concordance and adequate sampling. If discordant, escalate management [1].

Detailed lesion-specific guidance

Accordion format for quick scanning; each citation links to the bibliography.

Clinical nuance
ADH Default excision; selective observation in carefully chosen patients
Often excise
  • Standard: Surgical excision recommended [3][4]
  • Select patients may consider observation if: ≤2 foci of ADH; ≥90% of calcifications removed at biopsy; no necrosis or significant atypia; no enhancement at MRI; incidental finding [3][5]
  • Post-excision: Enhanced screening per NCCN (annual mammogram; consider MRI if ≥20% lifetime risk) [1]
Lobular neoplasia (ALH / classic LCIS) Observation acceptable when concordant and not extensive; non-classic LCIS excise
Risk-based
  • ALH: Observation acceptable if concordant and adequately sampled [4][11]
  • Classic LCIS: Observation acceptable if concordant, not extensive, not associated with calcifications [4][5]
  • Non-classic LCIS (pleomorphic/florid): Excision with negative margins recommended [1]
  • After diagnosis: Enhanced screening; consider risk reduction strategies [1]
FEA Pure FEA has low upgrade; observation possible with strict criteria
Often observe
  • Pure FEA upgrade rate: ~1.3–7% depending on study [12][13][14]
  • Observation may be considered if: pure FEA (no concurrent ADH); small lesion completely removed at biopsy; concordant imaging; no genetic mutation; no personal history of breast cancer [3][14][15]
  • If excised and benign: Return to routine screening [1]
Intraductal papilloma With atypia excise; without atypia use risk stratification
Selective excision
  • With atypia: Excision recommended [2][6]
  • Without atypia — excision recommended if: discordant; symptomatic (bloody nipple discharge); palpable; size ≥10 mm; age ≥60; >50% removed at biopsy; family history; ≥4 concurrent peripheral papillomas [2][16]
  • Without atypia — observation acceptable if: concordant; ≤1 cm; >50% removed; asymptomatic; age <60; no family history [16][17][18]
Radial scar / complex sclerosing lesion With atypia excise; without atypia consider observation if well sampled
Consider VAE
  • With atypia: Excision recommended (≈33% upgrade) [19]
  • Without atypia: Upgrade rate ~1–9% [19][20][21][22]
  • Observation may be considered if: concordant; adequately sampled (>50% of lesion); no associated atypia [9][21]
  • VAE: May be alternative to open surgery [9][10]
Fibroepithelial lesions / phyllodes Excision for FEL; growth criteria for fibroadenoma; phyllodes excision
Often excise
  • FEL on core biopsy: Excision recommended (25–35% upgrade to phyllodes) [8]
  • “FEL, concern for phyllodes”: Excision strongly recommended [8]
  • Benign phyllodes tumor: Excision recommended (continued growth; malignant potential) [8][10]
  • Concordant fibroadenoma: No routine excision needed [8]
  • Excise fibroadenoma if: discordant; symptoms/preference; size >4–6 cm; growth >20% in 6 months; any concern for phyllodes [8]

Key principles

What to emphasize in daily breast imaging practice.

Essentials
  1. Concordance is critical: Radiology–pathology correlation must be established for every biopsy [1]
  2. Multidisciplinary review: High-risk lesion management benefits from conference review [5][23]
  3. Upgrade rates have decreased: Larger-bore needles, modern imaging, and concordance emphasis have lowered upgrade rates for many lesions [11]
  4. Individualize decisions: Patient factors (age, family history, genetic mutations, prior breast cancer) should influence management [3][14][24]
  5. Enhanced surveillance after high-risk lesions: ADH, ALH, and LCIS increase lifetime risk and warrant enhanced screening [1][25]
